Title
For a presentation copy now contained in MS XI, Ruskin entitled the poem “Glen of Glenfarg” (beginning with the line “Papa how pretty those icicles are”). For a copy in MS III, perhaps because he noticed that he had already used that title for another poem in the same Red Book, “Glen of Glenfarg” (beginning with the line “Glen of Glenfarg thy beauteous rill”), he titled the MS III copy of the present poem “Glenfarg”. He wrote both titles in all capital letters.

Date
January 1.
Collingwood questions Ruskinʼs 1828 dating of the poem in Queen of the Air, reascribing it to 1 January 1827, “as proved by a copy in No. XI” (PJR [1891], 1:?; " target="_self">PJR [8o], 1:269). Cook and Wedderburn accept Collingwoodʼs conclusions somewhat vaguely, identifying no. 22 as clearly the 1828 New Yearʼs poem, while imagining no. 23 as having “been selected by the young author as the best specimen of his stock in hand”—a scenario that they then present as justification for their selecting no other work from 1827 for publication (" target="_self">Works 2:260 n. 1). All of this is mistaken, however. The copy in MS XI as presently bound is clearly dated “January 1 1828” in Ruskinʼs hand. Collingwood may have been confused by Ruskinʼs entitling the MS XI copy as “Glen of Glenfarg,” the same title as no. 2, instead of “Glenfarg” as in MS III. All copies now available of no. 23 confirm a New Yearʼs presentation of 1828.
